10 Microsoft 365 Security Mistakes Small Businesses Make

Many businesses rely on Microsoft 365 daily, but simple configuration mistakes can expose systems to cyber threats. Here are the most common security mistakes and how to avoid them.

Common Microsoft 365 Security Mistakes

  • Not enabling Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
    Without MFA, stolen passwords can give attackers access to email and company files.
  • Weak password policies
    Short or reused passwords make accounts easier to compromise.
  • No phishing protection
    Without proper email security, employees may click malicious links or attachments.
  • No backup for Microsoft 365 data
    Businesses often assume Microsoft automatically backs up everything.
  • Too many administrator accounts
    Excessive admin privileges increase the risk if an account is compromised.
  • No conditional access policies
    Accounts may be able to sign in from any device or location.
  • No monitoring of login activity
    Suspicious sign-ins can go unnoticed if logs are not checked regularly.
  • Poor file sharing controls
    OneDrive and SharePoint files may accidentally be shared publicly.
  • No employee security awareness training
    Users may fall victim to phishing or social engineering attacks.
  • No incident response plan
    Without a response plan, security incidents can cause greater downtime and damage.
