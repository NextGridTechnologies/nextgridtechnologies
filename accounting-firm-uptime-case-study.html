How We Killed 5 Hours of Monthly Downtime for an Accounting Firm

January 2026 · 5 min read · Client Story

"Every month-end our server dies." Cost: R12,000 in lost billable hours. Cause: Nobody monitored it. Here's how we fixed it in 90 days.

The Before: Break-Fix Chaos

❌ Before NextGrid

  • IT guy: "Just reboot the server"
  • No monitoring: Issues found when staff complained
  • No logs: Nobody knew why Pastel crashed monthly
  • Hardware: 8-year-old switch, 5-year-old server
  • Backup: External HDD, last tested 2019
  • Monthly cost: R8k break-fix + R12k lost time = R20k

✅ After NextGrid

  • 24/7 monitoring: Alert before staff notice
  • Root cause fixed: New switch + UPS
  • Logs centralised: Know exactly why crashes happen
  • Hardware: Business-grade, 3-year warranty
  • Backup: Daily cloud, tested quarterly
  • Monthly cost: R2,000 managed [10 users]

The R950 Audit Findings [23-Point Check]

  • Critical: Switch CRC errors — failing hardware causing network drops
  • Critical: No UPS on server — load-shedding = hard crashes, database corruption
  • High: 0% disk monitoring — C: drive at 98%, Pastel crashing from no space
  • High: Backups failing silently for 6 months — external HDD full
  • Medium: 15 unused M365 licenses = R1,800/mo wasted

The Fix: 90-Day Plan

  • Month 1: Replace switch [R4,000], add UPS [R3,000], setup PRTG monitoring
  • Month 2: Migrate Pastel to Azure VM, decommission old server
  • Month 3: Retire 15 licenses, enable disk alerts, document all passwords in client portal
  • Total cost: R15,000 once-off + R2,000/mo managed [10 users]

The After: 6 Months Zero Outages

  • Downtime: 5 hours/month → 0 hours
  • Billable hours recovered: R12,000/month
  • IT cost: R20,000/mo → R2,000/mo
  • ROI: 6x in first year
  • Client quote: "We didn't realize IT could be boring. That's the goal."

The 3 Root Causes We See Everywhere

  • 1. No monitoring: "If it ain't broke..." until month-end and it is
  • 2. Ancient hardware: "It still works" until it doesn't during SARS deadline
  • 3. No documentation: IT guy leaves, passwords die with him, nobody can login
